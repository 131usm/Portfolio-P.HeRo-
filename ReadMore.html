About

Winston Churchill was a British politician, statesman, writer, and orator, who was born on November 30, 1874, in Blenheim Palace, Oxfordshire, England. He came from a prominent aristocratic family, and his father was a politician and an author.

Also Known As: Sir Winston Leonard Spencer Churchill
Died At Age: 90
Spouse/Ex-: Clementine Churchill (M. 1908–1965)
Father: Lord Randolph Churchill
Siblings: John Strange Spencer-Churchill
Children: Diana Churchill, Marigold Churchill, Mary Soames, Randolph Churchill, Sarah Churchill
Political Ideology: Conservative (1900–04, 1924–64), Liberal (1904–24)
Died On: January 24, 1965
Place Of Death: London, England, United Kingdom
Notable Alumni: RMC Sandhurst, Harrow School
Cause Of Death: Stroke
Epitaphs: I Am Ready To Meet My Maker. Whether My Maker Is Prepared For The Great Ordeal Of Meeting Me Is Another Matter.

Churchill's political career began in 1900 when he was elected to the House of Commons as a Conservative Member of Parliament. He held a number of government positions over the course of his career, including Home Secretary, Chancellor of the Exchequer, and First Lord of the Admiralty. He also served as Prime Minister of the United Kingdom twice, from 1940 to 1945 and from 1951 to 1955. Churchill is perhaps best known for his leadership during World War II. He became Prime Minister shortly after the outbreak of war in 1939 and led the country through some of its darkest days. He rallied the British people and inspired them to persevere through the difficult times. His speeches and broadcasts, such as the famous "We shall fight on the beaches" speech, remain iconic to this day. Churchill was also a prolific writer and won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1953 for his contributions to the field. His books, including his six-volume history of World War II, are still widely read and studied.
